3. Review & Refinement

4. Final Proof & Delivery

Literary Translation vs. Direct Translation

Direct Translation (Hindi → English)

“वह नदी के किनारे बैठा था, सोच रहा था।”

Direct Output: “He was sitting by the river, thinking.”

✔ Correct meaning, but lacks atmosphere and emotional depth.

Literary Translation (Hindi → English)

“वह नदी के किनारे बैठा था, सोच रहा था।”

Literary Output: “He sat by the riverbank, lost in thought, as the waters carried his silence away.”

✔ Preserves mood, imagery, and the poetic rhythm of the original.
